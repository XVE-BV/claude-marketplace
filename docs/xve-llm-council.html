LLM Council

XVE · Get five independent AI perspectives on a high-stakes decision

Say council this, war room this, or pressure-test this — Claude convenes five advisors with different thinking styles, runs an anonymous peer review, then synthesises a Chairman's verdict with a concrete recommendation. Use it when the cost of a bad call is high.

When to Use It

Good fit

  • Genuine uncertainty with real stakes
  • Two or more options with meaningful tradeoffs
  • "Should I hire a VA or build automation first?"
  • "Should I launch at $97 or $497?"
  • Decision you keep going back and forth on

Not a good fit

  • Factual lookups — just ask Claude directly
  • Creation tasks — write a tweet, summarise this
  • Already decided — don't run the council to validate
  • Casual "should I" without real tradeoff

Why parallel advisors? If advisors run one after another, each one reads the previous responses and starts to agree. Running them all at once keeps their perspectives genuinely independent — that's where the value comes from.

The Five Advisors

The Contrarian

Actively looks for what's wrong, what's missing, what will fail. Not a pessimist — the friend who saves you from a bad deal by asking the questions you're avoiding. If everything looks solid, digs deeper.

The First Principles Thinker

Ignores the surface question and asks "what are we actually trying to solve?" Strips away assumptions, rebuilds from the ground up. Often the most valuable output is "you're asking the wrong question entirely."

The Expansionist

Looks for upside everyone else is missing. What could be bigger? What adjacent opportunity is hiding? Doesn't care about risk — that's the Contrarian's job. Focuses on what happens if this works even better than expected.

The Outsider

Has zero context about you, your field, or your history. Responds purely to what's in front of them. Catches the curse of knowledge: things obvious to you but confusing to everyone else.

The Executor

Only cares about one thing: can this be done, and what's the fastest path? Ignores theory and strategy. If an idea has no clear first step, says so.

What You Get at the End

Chairman's verdict

Not "it depends" — a direct recommendation with reasoning, plus the one thing to do first.

Where the council agrees

Converged points from multiple advisors = high-confidence signals you can trust.

Where the council clashes

Genuine disagreements surfaced so you can weigh them yourself — not smoothed over.

Blind spots caught

Things the peer review surfaced that no single advisor caught on their own.
